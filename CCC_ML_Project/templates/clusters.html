02Cluster 0

High Crime Metropolitan Cities

The most intense crime profile in the dataset. Dense urban centers where incident volume, weapon involvement, and police deployment all peak — yet closure rates fail to keep pace.

CLUSTER 0 · PATTERN ALPHA
High Crime Metropolitan
Dense Urban · Maximum Threat
Threat: HIGH
// Crime Profile Summary

Major metros with the nation's highest crime volumes. Total crimes are very high, weapon crime rates are significantly elevated, and police deployment is heavy — but case closure rates remain moderate, unable to keep pace with incident volume. Younger average victim age compared to other clusters.

// Characteristics
  • Very high total crime volume — primary PCA driver
  • Elevated weapon crime rate (~34% of incidents)
  • Highest police deployment per city
  • Moderate case closure rate (≈58%) despite heavy resources
  • Younger average victim age (28–34)
  • Tightly clustered in PCA — cities are behaviorally similar

03Cluster 1

Moderate Crime Cities

The largest cluster by population. Mid-tier urban centers with measurable but contained crime activity — balanced between enforcement resources and incident rate.

CLUSTER 1 · PATTERN BRAVO
Moderate Crime Cities
Tier-2 Urban · Stable Activity
Threat: MEDIUM
// Crime Profile Summary

Mid-sized cities with steady, mixed-category crime activity. No single feature dominates — total crimes are moderate, weapon rates are mixed, and police deployment is proportional. Case closure rates are stable. The most numerically populated cluster, accounting for 32% of all cities profiled.

// Characteristics
  • Moderate total crime volume
  • Mixed weapon crime rate (~20% of incidents)
  • Proportional police deployment
  • Stable case closure rate (≈63%)
  • Average victim age distribution (32–38)
  • Consistent enforcement response across cases
// Cities in This Cluster

04Cluster 2

Low Crime Cities

The benchmark cluster. Smaller jurisdictions with the lowest crime density in the dataset — minimal weapon involvement, high closure rates, and lean but effective enforcement.

CLUSTER 2 · PATTERN CHARLIE
Low Crime Cities
Smaller Urban · Contained Profile
Threat: LOW
// Crime Profile Summary

The safest cluster in the dataset. These smaller urban zones have the lowest total crime counts, minimal weapon involvement, and the highest case closure rates — suggesting efficient enforcement with far fewer resources. Older average victim age signals a different social exposure pattern entirely.

// Characteristics
  • Low total crime volume
  • Minimal weapon crime rate (~9% of incidents)
  • Lean but effective police deployment
  • Highest case closure rate (≈71%)
  • Older average victim age (36–44)
  • Consistent and stable enforcement throughput
// Cities in This Cluster
Chandigarh Indore Vadodara Coimbatore Kochi Thiruvananthapuram Mysore Rajkot
05Cluster 3

Outlier Cities

The anomaly cluster. Cities whose crime signatures resist classification with any primary centroid. Their patterns are genuinely unusual — and that divergence is itself the finding.

CLUSTER 3 · PATTERN DELTA
Outlier Cities
Anomalous · Manual Review Required
Threat: SPECIAL REVIEW
// Crime Profile Summary

Cities that the model flags as genuinely anomalous. High weapon rate without proportional crime volume, or unusual demographic concentrations, place these cities outside the three primary profiles. Their distance from all centroids in PCA space is the defining feature — requiring individual policy attention.

// Characteristics
  • Variable total crime volume — no dominant pattern
  • Anomalous weapon crime rate relative to volume
  • Irregular police deployment patterns
  • Inconsistent case closure rate across reporting periods
  • Unique demographic skew — victim age distribution atypical
  • Maximum centroid distance in PCA projection
